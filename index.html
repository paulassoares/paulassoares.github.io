About

Hi there! I'm a final year Cosmology and Data Science PhD student at Queen Mary University of London, supervised by Dr. Alkistis Pourtsidou. I work on neutral hydrogen intensity mapping, a novel and exciting way to map out the structure of the Universe. Since this technique is so new, it comes with a lot of challenges such as data cleaning, calibration, and modelling of systematic effects. The bulk of my research has been on how to minimize these challenges and maximize the amount of insight to be gained from the data. I am currently looking to move to the field of data science, and am eager to transfer my skills to the context of real world problems. In my free time, I enjoy weightlifting, cycling, baking, and watching so-bad-it's-good movies.

Code

I am proficient in Python in the context of cosmology and data science, and have developed and contributed to several GitHub-based code packages.

My focus recently has been on implementing Gaussian Process Regression as a data cleaning technique for the first time in low redshift intensity mapping. I published a user-friendly Python package for this method called gpr4im, which can be found at this link.

I also contributed to the the MultipoleExpansion Python package (found here), which can be used to calculate the power spectrum of intensity mapping data, as well as generating lognormal simulations and performing Markov Chain Monte Carlo analyses.
