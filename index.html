About

Hi there! I'm a final year Cosmology and Data Science PhD student at Queen Mary University of London, being supervised by Dr. Alkistis Pourtsidou. I work on neutral hydrogen intensity mapping, a novel and exciting way to map out the structure of the Universe. Since this technique is so new, it comes with a lot of challenges such as data cleaning, calibration, and modelling of systematic effects. The bulk of my research has been on how to minimize these challenges and maximize the amount of insight to be gained from these experiments. I am currently looking to move to the field of data science, and am eager to transfer my skills to the context of real world problems. In my free time, I enjoy weightlifting, cycling and playing guitar.
